{% endblock %} {% block steps %}

  1. Get professional advice and see the City of Chicago’s Urban Agriculture FAQ to determine how to get zoning approval for community agriculture.

  2. The City of Chicago defines Community Gardens differently from how it defines Urban Farms. Community Gardens are typically operated non-profit by volunteers, and cannot exceed 25,000 SF in area. Urban Farms are commercial operations with a business license.

  3. Certain “accessory buildings,” such as sheds, hoop houses, and greenhouses, require approval from Zoning, and possibly a permit. The size and materials used will determine what approval process you’ll need. You may need to hire an architect and/or general contractor for a permit. Get professional advice.

  4. Research and connect with nearby community gardens in your area through the Chicago Community Gardener Association (CCGA) to foster a strong volunteer team.
