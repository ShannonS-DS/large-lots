Key Resources

What opportunities and steps can help you with your lot, no matter what you’re planning to do next?

Gather your design ideas
One way to prepare for a meeting with your design professional is to gather images of spaces you like from popular sites like:

  • Houzz.com
  • Pinterest.com
  • ApartmentTherapy.com
  • DesignSponge.com
  • design-milk.com
  • witanddelight.com
  • dwell.com

Learn more about becoming a landlord
See the City of Chicago’s Residential Landlord and Tenant Ordinance.

Know your funding & loan options
See the Neighborhood Housing Services Chicago website.

Appeal your property taxes
See the Cook County Board of Review.

Apply for non-profit status
If you want to make your lot publicly accessible or seek tax-deductible donations, learn more through the Law Project and the City of Chicago Small Business Center.

Save money through sustainability
Contact Elevate Energy, a local non-profit of experts extremely dedicated to helping Chicago property owners save money by saving energy. www.elevateenergy.org, call 855.372.8377, or email buildings@elevateenergy.org.
Owners of buildings with 1-4 residential units may be eligible for Energy Efficiency Loan Options offered by the Chicago Community Loan Fund.
Free Energy Assessment: ComEd and Elevate Energy both offer free home energy assessment to help identify how to partner for savings. ComEd: 845-433-2700.

Find information about community gardening
Contact Chicago Community Gardener Association.

Learn about funding & loan options
See the Neighborhood Housing Services Chicago website.

Find out about dog parks
Chicago Park District dog park information: Search “Dog Friendly Area” on www.chicagoparkdistrict.com. For info: Claudine Malik, claudine.malik@chicagoparkdistrict.com.

Playground grants
Playground grants are sometimes available via Kaboom, a non-profit that partners with organizations and businesses serving charitable purposes to bring play to urban neighborhoods. Look for playground grant info as well as general advice on kaboom.org/grants.

Playground manufacturers
Recommended Playground Manufacturers: The playground companies typically used by the Chicago Park District include: Landscape Structures; GameTime; Kompan; and Dynamo. Be advised that all playgrounds must adhere to ASTM & CPSC rules. Soft surfacing is required by law. Commonly, this is similar to woodchips. Rubberized surfaces, while popular, may add hundreds of thousands to your budget and requires restoration in 3 years.

Get some design ideas
To get some design ideas: One way to prepare for a meeting with your architect is to gather images of interiors you like from sites such as: Houzz.com, Pinterest.com, ApartmentTherapy.com, DesignSponge.com, design-milk.com, witanddelight.com, and dwell.com

Building a pool
Request a free consultation with experienced Chicagoland companies like Barrington Pools, Downes Pool, or All Seasons Pools. There are many others.
Consider pre-packaged options: Retailers like The Great Escape and large home improvement oriented department stores can give you a sense of price for pre-packaged pool & hot tub units.

Ornamental ponds & fountains
For ornamental ponds + fountains: Find a Certified Aquascape Contractor serving Chicagoland like: Gem Ponds, Artistic Design Garden Ponds, or Aqua Landscapes. There are many others.

Porches and decks
To get a free estimate: Request a quote from Chicago Porch Builders, 773.251.9879
To get a free inspection by a Building Inspector: Call 3-1-1 and ask for an “owner-requested porch inspection.”

Outdoor furniture
The Chicago Park District commonly installs durable benches and other outdoor furniture from: Landscape Structures; GameTime; Dumor
