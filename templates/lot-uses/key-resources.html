Key Resources

What opportunities and steps can help you with your lot, no matter what you’re planning to do next?

Gather your design ideas
One way to prepare for a meeting with your design professional is to gather images of spaces you like from popular sites like:

  • Houzz.com
  • Pinterest.com
  • ApartmentTherapy.com
  • DesignSponge.com
  • design-milk.com
  • witanddelight.com
  • dwell.com

Learn more about becoming a landlord
See the City of Chicago’s Residential Landlord and Tenant Ordinance.

Know your funding & loan options
See the Neighborhood Housing Services Chicago website.

Appeal your property taxes
See the Cook County Board of Review.

Apply for non-profit status
If you want to make your lot publicly accessible or seek tax-deductible donations, learn more through the Law Project and the City of Chicago Small Business Center.

Save money through sustainability
Contact Elevate Energy, a local non-profit of experts extremely dedicated to helping Chicago property owners save money by saving energy. www.elevateenergy.org, call 855.372.8377, or email buildings@elevateenergy.org.

How to Find a Qualified Professional

Architect
AIA Chicago Architect Finder

General contractor

  • Associated Builders and Contractors Illinois Chapter Membership Directory
  • Building Performance Institute Certified Professionals
  • Angie’s List for Contractors

Make sure any contractor you are considering is licensed by checking the City of Chicago’s List of Registered Contractors: webapps1.cityofchicago.org/activegcWeb

Surveyor
Major local surveyors include, but are not limited to:

  • Chicagoland Survey
  • Central Survey
  • Sekerez Land Surveying

Grant writer
Search for experienced freelance writing professionals on

  • Puget Sound Grantwriters Association
  • Elance
  • Upwork
