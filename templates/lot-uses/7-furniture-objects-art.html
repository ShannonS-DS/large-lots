{% endblock %} {% block steps %}

  1. If you are considering these fixed objects for private use only, consult a design professional, as you may simply need an Easy Permit to proceed (or even no permit at all).

  2. You may need to hire a licensed general contractor, typically if your object (such as a post for a basketball hoop) requires some kind of foundation.

  3. If you plan to have the public onto your privately-owned land, talk to your homeowner’s insurance company about changes you will want to make in your policy to protect yourself from this change in liability.

  4. By establishing a community association for users, you may be able to reduce your liability if members of the association sign a waiver and/or contribute a membership fee to offset higher insurance costs. Talk to your insurer. Homeowners Associations have accomplished this in the past.
